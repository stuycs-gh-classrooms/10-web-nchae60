Zero Order Reactions

When graphed against the numeric concentration of the reactant with an x-axis of time, it will linearize.

First Order Reactions

When graphed with the same units of a zero order reaction, it will appear as a negative exponential graph.
Because a second order reaction appears the same, they must be distinguished by the y-axis they can be graphed against in order to achieve
a linear graph. In the case of a first-order reaction, graphing it against the natural log (ln) of the numeric concentration achieves linearization.


Source

Second Order Reactions

When graphed against the reciprocal of the numeric concentration, a second order reaction will linearize.
As stated prior, if these reactions are graphed against numeric concentration, the second and first order appear far too
similar to distinguish reliably.
